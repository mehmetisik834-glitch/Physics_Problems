# Trigonometry: Calculating Vector Components

## 1. Definitions and Formulas

### Vector Decomposition
Any vector in a 2D plane can be broken down into two perpendicular parts: a **horizontal component** ($A_x$) and a **vertical component** ($A_y$). This process is known as vector decomposition.

### Trigonometric Functions
We use the basic trigonometric ratios from a right-angled triangle to find these components:

1.  **Cosine ($\cos$):** Relates the adjacent side to the hypotenuse.
2.  **Sine ($\sin$):** Relates the opposite side to the hypotenuse.

### The Formulas

$$
A_x = A \cdot \cos(\theta)
$$

$$
A_y = A \cdot \sin(\theta)
$$

Where:
* **$A$**: The magnitude (length) of the vector.
* **$\theta$**: The angle the vector makes with the horizontal (x-axis).



---

## 2. Problem Statement

A vector $\vec{A}$ has:
* **Magnitude ($A$):** 15
* **Angle ($\theta$):** $60^\circ$

**Goal:** Calculate the horizontal component ($A_x$) and the vertical component ($A_y$).

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Identify the given values
From the problem description, we have:

$$
A = 15, \quad \theta = 60^\circ
$$

### Step 2: Calculate the horizontal component ($A_x$)
Using the cosine formula:

$$
A_x = 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ)
$$

We know from the unit circle that $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$ (or $1/2$):

$$
A_x = 15 \cdot 0.5 = 7.5
$$

### Step 3: Calculate the vertical component ($A_y$)
Using the sine formula:

$$
A_y = 15 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)
$$

We know from the unit circle that $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$:

$$
A_y = 15 \cdot 0.866 \approx 12.99
$$

---

## 4. Summary Table

---

## Final Answer

The horizontal component of the vector is **7.5** and the vertical component is approximately **12.99**.
