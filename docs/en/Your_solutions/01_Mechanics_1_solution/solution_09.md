## 9. Linear Momentum Comparison

### Problem Statement
Determine which object has greater momentum:
1.  **Fly:** $m = 2 \text{ g}$, $v = 10 \text{ m/s}$
2.  **Tennis Ball:** $m = 60 \text{ g}$, $v = 1 \text{ m/s}$

### 1. Momentum Formula
Momentum ($p$) is calculated as:

$$
p = m \cdot v
$$

### 2. Calculations (SI Units)
Mass must be in kilograms ($\text{kg}$).

**Fly Momentum:**

$$
p_{fly} = (0.002 \text{ kg}) \cdot (10 \text{ m/s}) = 0.02 \text{ kg} \cdot \text{m/s}
$$

**Tennis Ball Momentum:**

$$
p_{ball} = (0.060 \text{ kg}) \cdot (1 \text{ m/s}) = 0.06 \text{ kg} \cdot \text{m/s}
$$

### 3. Conclusion
The **tennis ball** has a greater momentum ($0.06 \text{ kg} \cdot \text{m/s}$) than the fly ($0.02 \text{ kg} \cdot \text{m/s}$). Despite its lower velocity, the significantly larger mass of the tennis ball results in a higher momentum value.
