# 4. Energy & Momentum

## Necessary definitions and formulas

### 1) Gravitational potential energy

$$
U = mgh
$$

- \(m\): mass  
- \(g\): gravitational acceleration (\(9.8 \text{ m/s}^2\))  
- \(h\): height  

---

### 2) Kinetic energy

$$
K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

---

### 3) Conservation of mechanical energy
If there is **no friction**, mechanical energy is conserved:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

This allows us to find the speed at the bottom of the track.

---

### 4) Momentum

$$
p = mv
$$

---

### 5) Conservation of momentum (collision)
For collisions (even if energy is not conserved), **momentum is conserved**:

$$
m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

---

### 6) Important note (perfectly inelastic collision)
If the objects **stick together**, the collision is called **perfectly inelastic**:
- Momentum is conserved
- Kinetic energy is NOT conserved

---

## Given

- First block:
  
$$
m_1 = 0.5 \text{ kg}
$$

- Height:

$$
h = 3.0 \text{ m}
$$

- Second block:

$$
m_2 = 1.5 \text{ kg}
$$

- Initially at rest:

$$
v_2 = 0
$$

- Gravity:

$$
g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

---

## Step 1: Find speed of the first block before collision

Use energy conservation:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Cancel \(m\):

$$
gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2
$$

Solve for \(v\):

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

---

### Substitute values:

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{2(9.8)(3.0)}
$$

$$
v_1 = \sqrt{58.8}
$$

$$
v_1 \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

## Step 2: Apply conservation of momentum

Before collision:

$$
p_{\text{initial}} = m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2
$$

Since \(v_2 = 0\):

$$
p_{\text{initial}} = m_1 v_1
$$

After collision (they stick together):

$$
p_{\text{final}} = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

---

### Set them equal:

$$
m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Solve for \(v_f\):

$$
v_f = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2}
$$

---

## Step 3: Substitute values

$$
v_f = \frac{0.5 \times 7.67}{0.5 + 1.5}
$$

$$
v_f = \frac{3.835}{2.0}
$$

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

## Final answer

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

## Short explanation
- First, the block converts **potential energy → kinetic energy** while sliding down.
- Then, during the collision, **momentum is conserved** (not energy).
- Because the second block is heavier, the final speed becomes much smaller.
