## 10. Kinematics of Point M

### Problem Statement
Point M moves in the $xy$-plane. To find the motion characteristics, we perform successive differentiations of the coordinate functions.

### 1. Position
The position at any time $t$ is given by:

$$
\vec{r}(t) = x(t)\hat{i} + y(t)\hat{j}
$$

### 2. Velocity Vector
The velocity is the first derivative of position:

$$
\vec{v}(t) = \frac{dx}{dt}\hat{i} + \frac{dy}{dt}\hat{j}
$$

The speed (magnitude) is:

$$
v = \sqrt{\left(\frac{dx}{dt}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{dy}{dt}\right)^2}
$$

### 3. Acceleration Vector
The acceleration is the second derivative of position:

$$
\vec{a}(t) = \frac{d^2x}{dt^2}\hat{i} + \frac{d^2y}{dt^2}\hat{j}
$$

The total acceleration magnitude is:

$$
a = \sqrt{\left(\frac{d^2x}{dt^2}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{d^2y}{dt^2}\right)^2}
$$
