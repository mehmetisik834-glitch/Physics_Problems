## 2. Range Optimization Proof

### Objective
Show that the maximum range $R(\theta)$ is achieved at a launch angle of $45^\circ$.

### The Range Formula
The horizontal range $R$ as a function of the launch angle $\theta$ is given by:

$$
R(\theta) = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}
$$

### Analytical Proof
To maximize $R(\theta)$, we examine the term $\sin(2\theta)$. Since $v_0$ and $g$ are constants, the range depends entirely on the sine component.

1.  **The Sine Constraint:** The maximum value of any sine function is $1$.
2.  **Setting the Argument:**
    
$$
\sin(2\theta) = 1
$$

3.  **Solving for $\theta$:**
    The sine function equals $1$ when the angle is $90^\circ$ (or $\pi/2$ radians).
    
$$
2\theta = 90^\circ
$$

$$
\theta = 45^\circ
$$

### Conclusion
For a projectile launched from ground level, the **maximum range** is achieved at an angle of **$45^\circ$**. At this angle, the trade-off between horizontal velocity ($v_0 \cos\theta$) and time of flight (determined by $v_0 \sin\theta$) is perfectly balanced.
